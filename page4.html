Cleaning

Oil paints are much more difficult to clean compared to acrylic paints. Acrylic paints are water based, which means they can be easily cleaned with water, whereas oil paints need paint thinner or turpentine in order to be cleaned. To clean brushes, you can use a jar of turpentine/paint thinner and dip your paintbrush into the liquid, which will disolve the paint. You can also clean oil paint off of a canvas. You can take a rag, pour some paint thinner on it, and rub the spot on the canvas; it may not fully take off the paint, but it will take the majority of it away. After you leave the jar for a few days, the oil paints will settle at the bottom of the jar and the paint thinner will be at the top (the two will seperate). You can reuse the paint thinner, and take a napkin and clean out the bottom of the jar for later use.
