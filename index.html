A Brief History

Even though oil paints were discovered to have been used in caves of the Bamian Valley around the 5th century A.D., oils weren't extremely popular until the 15th century in Northern Europe. Jan Van Eyck is arguably the father of the practice because of his experiments and techniques in the medium. Oil paint would go on to replace tempera paint, which was the most popular medium at the time. In fact, when one thinks about the art of the Renaissance, most of the masterpieces are made with oil paints.
